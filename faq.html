Frequently.asked.questions

Quick answers about readings, data, and coverage

Is this fortune-telling?

No. The site provides symbolic frameworks for reflection and planning. It does not predict events, outcomes, or luck.

Do I need to create an account?

No. Everything runs locally in your browser. No account, no upload, and no backend storage.

Is my data stored anywhere?

Only in your browser’s localStorage, if you choose to fill the forms. Clearing browser data removes it. Nothing is uploaded or shared.

Are the readings accurate?

Readings are symbolic and culturally derived. Use them as a planning lens, not as factual predictions. For real decisions, use verifiable sources.

Why Eastern and Western?

Both traditions are included so you can see two symbolic views of the same birthday. They overlap in some ideas and differ in others. Comparison is the point.

Can I use this on mobile?

Yes. The site is mobile-first. Use it on a phone or tablet the same way you would on desktop.

What does Do / Avoid mean?

It is a symbolic planning guide for the day. Use it as a gentle nudge, not a rulebook. Pick one small “Do” and one minor “Avoid” to test.

How accurate is the BaZi reading?

The reading is symbolic and culturally derived. It is useful for reflection and planning style, not factual prediction. If a result feels wrong, treat it as one lens among many.

How should beginners start?

Start with the Learn page, then try BaZi, then the weekly planner, and finish with Horoscope · East + West. Keep it simple and test one small idea each week.

Recommended reading list

Look for beginner introductions to BaZi, 12 Daily Officers, 9 Star Ki, and tropical Western astrology. Prefer summaries and counseling-oriented books. Avoid sources that promise guaranteed luck or fixed fate.
